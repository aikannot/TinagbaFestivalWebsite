WHAT IS TINAGBA FESTIVAL?


Tinagba Festival is a harvest-offering celebration which also coincides on
the feast of Our Lady of Lourdes. This hugely popular and all-out fun festival
is yearly celebrated by the Iriguenos in Iriga City, Camarines sur during the
month of February. This festival in Bicol is all about offering the first harvest
of crops and fruits. It is derived from the early rituals of the ancient Bicolanos
offering their harvest to their own Gods as a form of thanksgiving and to seek
favor for a bountiful harvest throughout the year.

The event features a long procession of floats that are being pulled by carabaos
or motored floats packed with the best of the province's products. It also features
performers in beautiful decorative costumes who dance around the city with
marching bands from different school and University that made the streets lively
and amazing.

Background of the Festival

In the early 70's, Jose Calleja Reyes, an Irigueno topnotch lawyer and businessman whose avocation is Bicol history, revived an old Bicol
thanksgiving ritual called the Tinagba. Tinagba came from the word "tagba" which means "first harvest offering".The Tinagba can be said to
have been the precursor of the mushrooming of festivals all over the counry after it was staged in 1974. What Reyes re-presented was the
Christianized version of the Tinagba, which during the Spanish time was used to be held during the Feast of Corpus Christi. Reyes opted to
time the holding of the Tinagba during the feast day of Our Lady of Lourdes which shrine he developed as part of his hotel complex.

The Tinagba begins with the beating of the patong, a hollowed wooden gong, which signals the start of the caravan of carabao-drawn carts
laden with farmers' harvest around the main streets of the city. It usually ends at the foot of the grotto of Our Lady of Lourdes, where during
the celebration of the Mass, a priest blesses the offerings which eventually get to be distributed to a prison houses, home of the aged, hospitals
and other recipients.

The Feast of Abundant Harvest

In my hometown in Iriga City, Philippines we celebrated Tinagba Festival. Tinagba came from the word "tagba" which means "first harvest offering". Tinagba Festival is a yearly celebration in our city, during the month of February. It is the reenactment of an old pagan ritual of offering the best produce of the land. The festival originally started from the rituals of the earliest Bicolanos who offered their harvest to their Gods a way of thanksgiving and requesting for a more abundant harvest.
